 2012 Ford Focus

The Ford Focus is a compact car (C-segment in Europe) manufactured by the Ford Motor Company and created under Alex Trotman's Ford 2000 plan, which aimed to globalize model development and sell one compact vehicle worldwide. The Focus was primarily designed by Ford of Europe's German and British teams. The Focus was released in July 1998 in Europe, succeeding the Ford Escort, and replaced the Ford Laser in Australia in 1999. It has been built in various locations across the world, including Germany, Brazil, Argentina, China, India, Thailand, and the United States. In 2011, the Focus was the best-selling car in Europe.

 2013 Ford Fusion

The Ford Fusion is a four-door, five-passenger mid-size sedan manufactured and marketed by Ford. Introduced for the 2006 model year, three generations of the Fusion have been produced in gasoline, gas/electric hybrid, and gas/plug-in electric hybrid variants. The Fusion is manufactured by Ford at the Hermosillo Stamping and Assembly Plant in Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico; and at the Louisville Assembly Plant in Louisville, Kentucky, United States. The Fusion has been awarded the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety's Top Safety Pick+ award every year since 2012.

 Ford Taurus

The Ford Taurus is an automobile manufactured by Ford in the United States. It is now in its sixth generation, which was introduced at the 2010 North American International Auto Show in Detroit, Michigan. The Taurus was originally introduced in 1985 for the 1986 model year. The Taurus was the first front-wheel drive full-size car produced by Ford in North America, and the first front-wheel drive car produced by an American automaker since the 1930s. The Taurus was also the first Ford to be designed with a front-wheel drive layout. The Taurus was initially marketed as a "four-door sedan" and later as a "four-door car" in the United States. The Taurus was also marketed as the Ford Mondeo in Europe and the Ford Thunderbird in China.

 2011 Ford Explorer

The Ford Explorer is a mid-size sport utility vehicle produced by the Ford Motor Company. Introduced in 1990 for the 1991 model year, the Explorer was the first four-door SUV produced by Ford in North America. The Explorer went on to become one of the most popular sport utility vehicles on the road. The Ford Explorer was the best-selling SUV in the United States for 13 years and the best-selling truck in the United States for 23 years. The Explorer was the first SUV produced with a four-door body. It is currently in its fourth generation. The Explorer was designed with the traditional body-on-frame construction. The Explorer is manufactured in Louisville, Kentucky, United States; Oakville, Ontario, Canada; and Toluca, Mexico.
